## Retention Philosophy

### Our Commitment to People
At DocuHarmonize, we believe that retaining top talent is essential to our success as we scale from a startup to an enterprise-level organization. Our retention strategy focuses on creating an environment where people can do their best work, grow their careers, and contribute meaningfully to our mission.

### Retention Principles

#### 1. Growth-Oriented Culture
- **Career development**: Clear paths for advancement and skill development
- **Learning opportunities**: Continuous learning and professional development
- **Stretch assignments**: Challenging projects that build capabilities
- **Internal mobility**: Opportunities to explore different roles and functions

#### 2. Meaningful Work
- **Mission alignment**: Connecting daily work to company mission and impact
- **Autonomy**: Trust in how employees accomplish their goals
- **Mastery**: Opportunities to develop expertise and specialization
- **Purpose**: Understanding how individual contributions matter

#### 3. Competitive Total Rewards
- **Market compensation**: Competitive salary and benefits packages
- **Equity participation**: Meaningful ownership in company success
- **Performance recognition**: Rewards tied to individual and team achievements
- **Holistic benefits**: Supporting whole-person well-being

#### 4. Inclusive Environment
- **Psychological safety**: Environment where people feel safe to speak up and take risks
- **Belonging**: Everyone feels valued and included regardless of background
- **Fair treatment**: Equitable opportunities and treatment for all employees
- **Diverse perspectives**: Valuing different viewpoints and experiences

### Retention Metrics and Targets

#### Key Performance Indicators
- **Overall turnover**: Target `<15%` annual voluntary turnover
- **Regrettable turnover**: Target `<10%` (loss of high-performing employees)
- **Early tenure turnover**: Target `<5%` turnover in first year
- **Promotion rate**: Target >25% internal promotion rate annually
- **Employee satisfaction**: Target >85% satisfaction in engagement surveys

#### Segmented Analysis
- **Performance-based**: Track retention by performance rating
- **Tenure-based**: Monitor retention patterns by length of service
- **Role-based**: Analyze retention differences across functions
- **Demographics**: Ensure equitable retention across all groups

## Career Progression Framework

### Career Ladder Philosophy

Our career progression framework provides multiple pathways for growth, recognizing that people have different aspirations and strengths. We support both individual contributor and management tracks with clear advancement criteria.

### Individual Contributor Track

#### Engineering Career Ladder

**Software Engineer I (0-2 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Execute assigned tasks with guidance and mentorship
- **Skills**: Basic programming skills, willingness to learn
- **Impact**: Individual task completion
- **Progression criteria**: Demonstrates competence in core technologies

**Software Engineer II (2-4 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Own feature development with minimal guidance
- **Skills**: Proficient in multiple technologies, good problem-solving
- **Impact**: Feature-level impact
- **Progression criteria**: Consistently delivers quality work, helps junior developers

**Senior Software Engineer (4-8 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Lead complex projects, mentor junior engineers
- **Skills**: Expert in core technologies, system design capabilities
- **Impact**: Project-level impact, influences architecture decisions
- **Progression criteria**: Technical leadership, mentoring others, system ownership

**Principal Software Engineer (8-12 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Drive technical strategy, cross-team collaboration
- **Skills**: Deep technical expertise, excellent communication
- **Impact**: Multi-team impact, influences technical direction
- **Progression criteria**: Technical vision, cross-functional leadership, innovation

**Distinguished Engineer (12+ years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Company-wide technical influence, external thought leadership
- **Skills**: Industry expertise, strategic thinking
- **Impact**: Company-wide technical influence
- **Progression criteria**: Industry recognition, significant technical contributions

#### Product Career Ladder

**Associate Product Manager (0-2 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Support product initiatives, conduct research
- **Skills**: Basic product skills, analytical thinking
- **Impact**: Feature support and analysis

**Product Manager (2-5 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Own product features, work with engineering and design
- **Skills**: Product strategy, user research, data analysis
- **Impact**: Feature-level product decisions

**Senior Product Manager (5-8 years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Lead product areas, cross-functional collaboration
- **Skills**: Strategic thinking, leadership, technical depth
- **Impact**: Product area ownership

**Principal Product Manager (8+ years)**
- **Responsibilities**: Product strategy, multiple product areas
- **Skills**: Market expertise, strategic vision
- **Impact**: Product portfolio influence

### Management Track

#### Engineering Management Ladder

**Engineering Team Lead (3+ years IC experience)**
- **Responsibilities**: Technical leadership with some people management
- **Team size**: 2-3 direct reports
- **Skills**: Technical expertise, basic management skills
- **Development**: Management training program

**Engineering Manager (5+ years experience)**
- **Responsibilities**: Full people management, project delivery
- **Team size**: 4-8 direct reports
- **Skills**: People development, project management, technical depth
- **Development**: Advanced management training

**Senior Engineering Manager (7+ years experience)**
- **Responsibilities**: Multiple teams or complex single team
- **Team size**: 8-15 direct reports (including managers)
- **Skills**: Organizational design, strategic thinking
- **Development**: Executive leadership development

**Director of Engineering (10+ years experience)**
- **Responsibilities**: Functional leadership, strategy development
- **Team size**: 15-50 team members
- **Skills**: Strategic vision, organizational leadership
- **Development**: Executive coaching and development

## Stock Option Program

### Equity Philosophy

#### Ownership Culture
We believe that all employees should have the opportunity to participate in the company's success through meaningful equity ownership. Our stock option program is designed to align individual success with company performance and create long-term retention incentives.

#### Program Objectives
- **Retention**: Long-term vesting schedules encourage tenure
- **Performance**: Refresh grants tied to individual performance
- **Alignment**: All employees benefit from company success
- **Fairness**: Equitable distribution based on role and contribution

### Stock Option Grants

#### Initial Grants (New Hires)
- **Eligibility**: All full-time employees after 90-day probationary period
- **Grant sizes**: Based on role, level, and market benchmarks
- **Vesting**: 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff
- **Strike price**: Fair market value at grant date

#### Grant Size Guidelines
| Role Level | Options Range | Typical Grant |
|------------|---------------|---------------|
| Entry Level | 1,000-5,000 | 2,500 |
| Mid Level | 3,000-10,000 | 6,000 |
| Senior Level | 8,000-20,000 | 12,000 |
| Principal/Staff | 15,000-35,000 | 25,000 |
| Director | 25,000-50,000 | 35,000 |
| VP | 40,000-80,000 | 60,000 |

#### Annual Refresh Grants
- **Performance-based**: Tied to individual performance ratings
- **Retention-focused**: Larger grants for key talent at risk
- **Market competitive**: Benchmarked against similar companies
- **Vesting schedule**: 3-year vesting for refresh grants

### Vesting and Exercise

#### Vesting Schedule
- **Initial grants**: 25% after 1 year, then 1/36th monthly
- **Refresh grants**: Monthly vesting over 3 years
- **Acceleration**: Partial acceleration upon acquisition
- **Departure**: Vested options exercisable for 90 days post-departure

#### Exercise Process
- **Exercise window**: 10-year option term from grant date
- **Payment methods**: Cash payment or cashless exercise
- **Tax implications**: Guidance provided, individual tax advice recommended
- **Administrative support**: HR assistance with exercise process

#### Early Exercise Program
- **Eligibility**: Available for all option grants
- **Benefits**: Start capital gains treatment earlier
- **Requirements**: File 83(b) election with tax authorities
- **Risk**: Potential loss of investment if company fails

### Equity Value Communication

#### Valuation Education
- **409A valuations**: Annual third-party valuations
- **Value drivers**: Communication about factors affecting company value
- **Market comparables**: Information about comparable company exits
- **Scenario modeling**: Potential value under different exit scenarios

#### Regular Communication
- **Quarterly updates**: Business performance and equity value drivers
- **Annual equity review**: Individual grant review and planning
- **Educational sessions**: Understanding equity compensation
- **One-on-one support**: HR availability for individual questions

## Performance Management

### Performance Philosophy

#### Continuous Performance Management
We believe that performance management should be an ongoing conversation rather than an annual event. Our approach focuses on frequent feedback, goal alignment, and development support to help everyone perform at their best.

#### Performance Principles
- **Forward-looking**: Focus on future development rather than past judgment
- **Growth-oriented**: Performance discussions center on growth and improvement
- **Fair and objective**: Consistent standards and evaluation criteria
- **Development-focused**: Performance management drives career development

### Performance Review Cycle

#### Quarterly Performance Conversations
- **Goal review**: Progress against quarterly OKRs and annual goals
- **Performance feedback**: Specific feedback on recent performance
- **Development discussion**: Progress on development goals and next steps
- **Support needs**: Identification of resources and support needed

#### Annual Performance Review
- **Comprehensive assessment**: Full evaluation of annual performance
- **360-degree feedback**: Input from multiple stakeholders
- **Calibration process**: Cross-team calibration for consistency
- **Career development planning**: Long-term career and development planning

#### Mid-Year Check-In
- **Progress assessment**: Mid-year evaluation of goal progress
- **Course correction**: Adjustments to goals and development plans
- **Recognition**: Acknowledgment of achievements and contributions
- **Support identification**: Additional support needs for second half

### Performance Rating System

#### Rating Scale and Distribution
1. **Exceptional (5%)**: Significantly exceeds all expectations
2. **Exceeds (20%)**: Consistently exceeds expectations
3. **Meets (65%)**: Fully meets all role expectations
4. **Developing (10%)**: Some improvement needed to meet expectations
5. **Unsatisfactory (0%)**: Does not meet basic role requirements

#### Rating Criteria

**Results Achievement (40%)**
- **Goal completion**: Achievement of quarterly and annual objectives
- **Quality of work**: Excellence and accuracy in deliverables
- **Impact measurement**: Measurable contribution to team and company success
- **Initiative**: Proactive identification and execution of improvements

**Behavioral Competencies (30%)**
- **Collaboration**: Effective teamwork and cross-functional partnership
- **Communication**: Clear, timely, and professional communication
- **Problem-solving**: Creative and effective approach to challenges
- **Adaptability**: Flexibility and resilience in changing environment

**Learning and Growth (20%)**
- **Skill development**: Progress on technical and professional skills
- **Development goals**: Achievement of individual development objectives
- **Learning application**: Application of new learning to work responsibilities
- **Knowledge sharing**: Contribution to team and company learning

**Culture and Values (10%)**
- **Values demonstration**: Living DocuHarmonize values in daily work
- **Cultural contribution**: Positive impact on team culture and environment
- **Inclusion**: Supporting inclusive and respectful workplace
- **Integrity**: Ethical behavior and decision-making

### Performance Improvement

#### Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
- **Triggers**: "Developing" or "Unsatisfactory" performance ratings
- **Duration**: 90-day improvement period with clear milestones
- **Support**: Weekly manager check-ins, additional resources, training
- **Success criteria**: Specific, measurable improvement goals
- **Outcomes**: Successful improvement, role change, or termination

#### Success Factors for PIPs
- **Clear expectations**: Specific behavioral and performance changes required
- **Regular feedback**: Weekly progress conversations and support
- **Resource provision**: Additional training, tools, or support provided
- **Documentation**: Detailed record of progress and support provided
