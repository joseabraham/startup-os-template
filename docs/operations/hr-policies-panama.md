## Legal Framework

### Applicable Laws
- **Código de Trabajo de Panamá (Labor Code)**
- **Ley 44 de 1995** (Labor Code modifications)
- **Decreto Ejecutivo No. 252 de 2020** (Telework regulations)
- **Ley 51 de 2005** (Equal opportunities)
- **CSS (Caja de Seguro Social)** requirements

### Regulatory Compliance
All employment practices must comply with:
- Ministry of Labor and Workforce Development (MITRADEL)
- Social Security Fund (CSS)
- Tax Authority (DGI)
- Immigration Service (SNM) for foreign workers

## Employment Contracts

### Contract Types

#### 1. Indefinite Term Contracts (Recommended)
- **Standard for all full-time employees**
- **Probationary period**: Maximum 3 months
- **Benefits**: Full labor law protections
- **Termination**: Requires just cause or severance

#### 2. Fixed-Term Contracts
- **Maximum duration**: 1 year (renewable once)
- **Use cases**: Project-based work, temporary replacements
- **Conversion**: Automatically becomes indefinite if renewed

#### 3. Professional Services Contracts
- **For independent contractors and consultants**
- **No labor law protections**
- **Clear scope definition required**
- **Quarterly review to prevent misclassification**

### Employment Classifications

#### Full-Time Employees
- **Minimum hours**: 40 hours/week (8 hours/day)
- **Maximum hours**: 48 hours/week without overtime
- **All statutory benefits apply**

#### Part-Time Employees
- **Less than 40 hours/week**
- **Proportional benefits**
- **Same hourly protections**

### Foreign Worker Requirements
- **Work permits** required for non-Panamanian citizens
- **Quota compliance**: 90% Panamanian, 10% foreign for most positions
- **Exemptions**: Management, technical specialists, investors
- **Documentation**: Passport, work permit, residence permit

## Working Hours and Overtime

### Standard Working Hours
- **Regular week**: 40 hours (Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM)
- **Flexible hours**: Core hours 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM (for tech roles)
- **Lunch break**: 1 hour (unpaid)
- **Remote work**: Same hour requirements apply

### Overtime Regulations
- **Overtime threshold**: More than 8 hours/day or 40 hours/week
- **Overtime rate**: 25% above regular hourly rate
- **Night work**: 25% premium (6:00 PM - 6:00 AM)
- **Holiday work**: 50% premium plus day off
- **Maximum overtime**: 3 hours/day, 12 hours/week

### Holiday Schedule
#### National Holidays (Paid)
- New Year's Day (January 1)
- Martyrs' Day (January 9)
- Carnival Monday and Tuesday (February/March)
- Good Friday (March/April)
- Labor Day (May 1)
- Separation Day (November 3)
- Flag Day (November 4)
- Independence Day (November 28)
- Mother's Day (December 8)
- Christmas Day (December 25)

#### Company Holidays
- **Floating holidays**: 2 additional days per year
- **Year-end closure**: December 26-31 (paid)

## Leave Policies

### Vacation Leave (Vacaciones)
- **Accrual rate**: 30 calendar days per year after 11 months
- **Minimum usage**: Must take at least 15 consecutive days annually
- **Payment**: Regular salary plus 13th month proportional
- **Carryover**: Up to 15 days to following year
- **Cash out**: Only upon termination

### Sick Leave (Incapacidad)
- **Short-term**: First 3 days paid by employer (100%)
- **Extended**: Day 4+ paid by CSS (60-70% of salary)
- **Maximum**: 26 weeks per condition
- **Documentation**: Medical certificate required after day 1
- **Mental health**: Covered under general sick leave

### Maternity Leave (Licencia de Maternidad)
- **Duration**: 14 weeks total
  - 2 weeks pre-birth (optional)
  - 12 weeks post-birth (mandatory)
- **Payment**: 100% salary (CSS reimburses employer)
- **Job protection**: Position guaranteed upon return
- **Additional benefits**:
  - 1 hour daily for breastfeeding (6 months)
  - Flexible return schedule available

### Paternity Leave (Licencia de Paternidad)
- **Duration**: 15 calendar days
- **Payment**: 100% salary
- **Timing**: Within 30 days of birth/adoption
- **Documentation**: Birth certificate required

### Personal Leave
- **Bereavement**: 3 days for immediate family, 1 day for extended family
- **Marriage**: 5 consecutive days
- **Education**: Time off for professional development (unpaid)
- **Jury duty**: Paid leave as required
- **Military service**: As required by law

### Leave Without Pay
- **Personal reasons**: Up to 30 days per year (manager approval)
- **Extended sick leave**: After CSS benefits exhausted
- **Family care**: Elder or child care responsibilities

## Compensation and Benefits

### Salary Structure
- **Base salary**: Market-competitive rates in USD
- **13th month bonus**: Mandatory (December salary equivalent)
- **Performance bonus**: Discretionary based on company and individual performance
- **Equity participation**: Stock options for all employees

### Benefits Package

#### Mandatory Benefits (Required by Law)
- **Social Security (CSS)**: 9.75% employee, 12.25% employer
- **Educational Insurance**: 1.5% employer contribution
- **Occupational Risk Insurance**: 0.4-6% employer (based on risk level)

#### Company Benefits
- **Health Insurance**: Private medical coverage (100% company paid)
- **Life Insurance**: 2x annual salary
- **Professional development**: $2,000 annual budget per employee
- **Technology stipend**: $1,000 annual for home office setup
- **Internet allowance**: $100/month for remote workers
- **Gym membership**: $100/month reimbursement
- **Meal vouchers**: $10/day for office days

### Compensation Philosophy
- **Market positioning**: 75th percentile of local market + equity upside
- **Pay equity**: Regular audits to ensure fair compensation
- **Transparency**: Salary bands published internally
- **Performance-based**: Merit increases tied to performance reviews

### Stock Options
- **Eligibility**: All employees after 90-day probationary period
- **Vesting schedule**: 4-year vesting, 1-year cliff
- **Exercise price**: Fair market value at grant date
- **Acceleration**: Partial acceleration upon acquisition/IPO

## Performance Management

### Performance Review Cycle
- **Annual reviews**: Comprehensive performance assessment (December)
- **Mid-year check-ins**: Progress review and goal adjustment (June)
- **Quarterly 1:1s**: Manager-employee development discussions
- **Continuous feedback**: Ongoing coaching and recognition

### Performance Rating Scale
1. **Exceptional (5%)**: Significantly exceeds expectations
2. **Exceeds (20%)**: Consistently above expectations
3. **Meets (60%)**: Fully meets role expectations
4. **Developing (15%)**: Some improvement needed
5. **Unsatisfactory (0%)**: Immediate improvement required

### Goal Setting Framework (OKRs)
- **Company OKRs**: Set quarterly, cascade to teams
- **Individual OKRs**: Aligned with company and role expectations
- **Measurement**: Quantitative and qualitative metrics
- **Review frequency**: Monthly progress updates

### Career Development
- **Individual Development Plans (IDPs)**: Created during annual reviews
- **Skill mapping**: Technical and soft skills assessment
- **Mentorship program**: Internal mentor assignments
- **Cross-training**: Exposure to different functions
- **Conference attendance**: Industry events and training

### Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
- **Triggers**: "Developing" or "Unsatisfactory" rating
- **Duration**: 90-day improvement period
- **Support**: Weekly manager check-ins, additional resources
- **Outcomes**: Improvement, role change, or termination

## Disciplinary Procedures

### Progressive Discipline Policy
DocuHarmonize follows a progressive discipline approach while maintaining legal compliance with Panama labor laws.

#### Step 1: Verbal Warning
- **Documentation**: Conversation notes in employee file
- **Timeline**: Immediate for minor infractions
- **Manager involvement**: Direct supervisor

#### Step 2: Written Warning
- **Format**: Formal written notice with improvement expectations
- **Employee signature**: Acknowledgment required
- **HR involvement**: Copy to HR file

#### Step 3: Suspension
- **Duration**: 1-3 days without pay (as allowed by law)
- **Serious infractions**: Safety violations, harassment
- **Investigation**: Full documentation required

#### Step 4: Termination
- **Just cause**: For serious misconduct
- **Process**: HR and legal review required
- **Documentation**: Complete record of progressive steps

### Serious Misconduct (Immediate Termination)
- Theft or fraud
- Workplace violence or threats
- Illegal drug or alcohol use
- Harassment or discrimination
- Breach of confidentiality
- Insubordination

### Employee Rights
- **Representation**: Union representative or advocate (if applicable)
- **Appeal process**: Right to appeal disciplinary actions
- **Due process**: Fair hearing before termination
- **Documentation access**: Right to review personnel file

## Remote Work Policies

### Eligibility and Approval
- **Default policy**: Remote-first for technology roles
- **Approval process**: Manager and HR approval required
- **Trial period**: 90-day evaluation period
- **Performance standards**: Same as office-based work

### Work Arrangements
- **Fully remote**: No office requirement
- **Hybrid**: 2-3 days office, 2-3 days remote
- **Temporary remote**: Situation-based (illness, family)
- **International remote**: Special approval required

### Technology Requirements
- **Equipment provision**: Company laptop, monitor, peripherals
- **Internet standards**: Minimum 25 Mbps download speed
- **Security requirements**: VPN, antivirus, encryption
- **Communication tools**: Slack, Zoom, project management tools

### Performance and Accountability
- **Output-based measurement**: Results over hours worked
- **Regular check-ins**: Weekly 1:1s with manager
- **Team collaboration**: Required participation in team meetings
- **Documentation**: Clear record of work completed

### Home Office Stipend
- **Setup allowance**: $1,000 one-time payment
- **Monthly internet**: $100 monthly reimbursement
- **Equipment refresh**: Every 3 years
- **Ergonomic support**: Chair and desk allowance

### Legal and Tax Implications
- **Work permits**: Required for international remote workers
- **Tax obligations**: Employee responsible for local tax compliance
- **Labor law jurisdiction**: Panama law applies to all employees
- **Data protection**: GDPR and local privacy law compliance

## Termination Procedures

### Voluntary Resignation
- **Notice period**:
  - Non-management: 2 weeks
  - Management: 4 weeks
- **Final pay**: Last day worked plus accrued benefits
- **Benefits continuation**: COBRA-equivalent health coverage
- **Knowledge transfer**: Required handover process

### Involuntary Termination

#### Termination with Just Cause
- **No severance**: Employee loses right to severance pay
- **Immediate effect**: No notice period required
- **Documentation**: Complete evidence file required
- **Legal review**: Attorney consultation mandatory

#### Termination without Just Cause
- **Severance calculation**:
  - 1-2 years: 1 month salary + proportional benefits
  - 2-10 years: 45 days salary + proportional benefits
  - 10+ years: 60 days salary + proportional benefits
- **Notice period**: 30 days or pay in lieu
- **Accrued benefits**: Vacation, 13th month, bonuses

### Severance Benefits
- **Severance pay**: As calculated above
- **Vacation payout**: All accrued unused vacation
- **13th month**: Proportional to months worked
- **Health insurance**: 3 months continuation coverage
- **Stock options**: Accelerated vesting per equity plan

### Exit Process
- **Exit interview**: HR-conducted feedback session
- **Asset return**: Company equipment and materials
- **System access**: Immediate revocation of all access
- **Knowledge transfer**: Comprehensive handover
- **Reference policy**: Neutral reference confirmation

### Legal Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Just cause documentation (if applicable)
- [ ] Severance calculation verification
- [ ] Final pay calculation including all benefits
- [ ] MITRADEL notification (mass layoffs)
- [ ] CSS notification of termination
- [ ] Final settlement signature
- [ ] Asset recovery completion
- [ ] System access revocation
