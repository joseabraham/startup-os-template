## Panama Labor Code (C√≥digo de Trabajo)

### Overview of Panama Labor Law

**Legal Framework:**
- Labor Code: Primary employment law
- Executive decrees and regulations
- Ministry of Labor (MITRADEL) oversight
- Labor Courts for dispute resolution
- Strong employee protections (civil law system)

**Key Principles:**
- Presumption in favor of employee (pro operario)
- Continuous employment presumed (indefinite contracts default)
- Mandatory benefits cannot be waived
- Written contracts required within 15 days
- Termination requires just cause or severance payment
- Collective bargaining rights protected

### Types of Employment Contracts

**1. Indefinite Contract (Contrato por Tiempo Indefinido)**

**Most Common for Startups:**
- No specified end date
- Continues until terminated by either party
- Provides maximum job security
- Full mandatory benefits apply
- Severance required if terminated without cause
- Can be terminated with just cause (no severance)

**When to Use:**
- Permanent hires (engineers, sales, operations)
- Core team members
- Standard for most positions
- Default if no contract type specified

**2. Fixed-Term Contract (Contrato por Tiempo Definido)**

**Limited Use Cases:**
- Maximum 1 year (renewable up to 3 years total)
- Must have specific project or temporary need
- Cannot be used for permanent positions
- If renewed >3 years, converts to indefinite
- Full benefits still apply
- No severance if ends at term (severance if terminated early)

**When to Use:**
- Specific projects with clear end date
- Seasonal work (rare in tech)
- Temporary replacement (maternity leave coverage)
- Trial for specific initiative

**Warning:** Misusing fixed-term contracts can result in automatic conversion to indefinite + penalties

**3. Task-Based Contract (Contrato por Obra Determinada)**

**Project-Specific:**
- Ends when specific work/project completed
- Common in construction, not typical for tech
- Clear deliverable must be defined
- Full benefits apply during project
- Can be longer than 1 year if project requires

**When to Use (Rare in Tech):**
- Specific software project with clear deliverable
- Implementation project for client
- Must be genuine task-based, not ongoing role

**4. Contractor/Freelancer (Prestaci√≥n de Servicios Profesionales)**

**Not an Employee:**
- Independent contractor relationship
- No employer-employee relationship
- No mandatory benefits required
- 10% income tax withholding (or 2% for professionals)
- No CSS contributions from company
- Must be genuinely independent

**True Contractor Criteria:**
- Provides services to multiple clients
- Uses own tools and equipment
- Controls own schedule and methods
- Invoices for services
- Bears own business risk
- Not integrated into company operations

**Warning:** Misclassified contractors = treated as employees + back pay + penalties + fines

### Mandatory Employee Benefits (Non-Negotiable)

**1. 13th Month Salary (D√©cimo Tercer Mes / Aguinaldo)**

**What It Is:**
- Mandatory bonus = 1/3 of annual salary
- Calculation: Total annual wages earned √∑ 3
- Paid in 3 equal installments:
  - April 15 (covers Jan 1 - Apr 15)
  - August 15 (covers Apr 16 - Aug 15)
  - December 15 (covers Aug 16 - Dec 15)

**Calculation Example:**
```

- April 15: $4,000 (3.5 months worked)

- December 15: $4,000 (4.5 months worked)

```

**Key Rules:**
- Includes all wages: base, bonuses, commissions
- Cannot be waived or negotiated
- Accrues from first day of employment
- Pro-rated for partial years
- Must be paid even if employee resigns
- Failure to pay = 3x the amount owed + penalties

**2. Vacation (Vacaciones)**

**Entitlement:**
- 30 calendar days per year
- After completing 11 months of continuous service
- Paid at regular salary rate
- Employee chooses timing (with reasonable notice)
- Can accumulate up to 60 days maximum
- Unused vacation paid upon termination

**Calculation:**
```
Monthly salary: $3,000
Daily rate: $3,000 √∑ 30 = $100/day
Vacation pay (30 days): $3,000

Employee works 6 months then resigns:
Accrued vacation: (30 days √∑ 12 months) √ó 6 = 15 days
Vacation pay owed: 15 days √ó $100 = $1,500
```

**Key Rules:**
- Cannot be paid in lieu (except at termination)
- Employer can deny specific dates (operational needs)
- Split vacation allowed (minimum 7 days at once)
- Accrues monthly: 2.5 days per month
- Must use within 2 years or forfeit excess over 60 days

**3. Severance Pay (Preaviso y Antig√ºedad)**

Only required for **termination without just cause**

**A. Preaviso (Notice Pay):**
- 1 month salary after 2 years employment
- 2 months salary after 5+ years employment
- Can work notice period or receive pay in lieu
- Based on final month's salary

**B. Antig√ºedad (Seniority Pay):**
- 1 week salary per year of service
- Starts after 2 years employment
- Maximum: 12 weeks
- Based on average salary of last 6 months

**Calculation Example:**
```
Employee: 5 years of service
Final salary: $4,000/month
Average last 6 months: $3,800/month

Preaviso: 2 months √ó $4,000 = $8,000
Antig√ºedad: 5 weeks √ó ($3,800 √∑ 4.33) = $4,390
Total Severance: $12,390

Plus: Accrued vacation, 13th month, any unpaid wages
```

**Important:**
- NOT required if:
  - Employee resigns voluntarily
  - Termination with just cause
  - Fixed-term contract ends naturally
- Must be paid immediately upon termination
- Failure to pay = employee can claim 125% + legal costs

**4. Social Security (CSS)**

**Contributions:**
- Employer: 12.25% of gross salary
- Employee: 9.75% of gross salary
- Total: 22% to CSS

**Educational Insurance (Seguro Educativo):**
- Employer: 1.25% of gross salary
- Employee: 1.25% of gross salary
- Total: 2.5%

**Work Risk Insurance (Riesgo Profesional):**
- Employer: 1.44% of gross salary (varies by industry)
- Employee: 0%

**Total Payroll Tax Burden:**
- Employer: 14.94%
- Employee: 11.00%
- Total: 25.94% of gross salary

**Registration:**
- Employer must register with CSS
- Each employee registered within 3 days of hire
- Monthly contributions due by 15th
- Late payment = 3% monthly penalty + interest

### Just Cause Termination (No Severance)

**Valid Reasons Under Labor Code:**

**1. Dishonesty or Misconduct:**
- Theft or fraud
- Falsification of documents (including r√©sum√©)
- Disclosure of confidential information
- Competing against employer
- Accepting bribes or kickbacks

**2. Violence or Harassment:**
- Physical violence against employer, coworkers, clients
- Verbal abuse or threats
- Sexual harassment
- Workplace bullying

**3. Insubordination:**
- Repeated refusal to follow lawful instructions
- Refusal to perform assigned duties
- Serious disrespect toward supervisor

**4. Intoxication:**
- Reporting to work intoxicated (alcohol or drugs)
- Drug use during work hours
- Repeated intoxication issues

**5. Abandonment:**
- Absence without notice for 2+ consecutive days
- After warning, repeated absences
- Leaving work without permission

**6. Poor Performance (Difficult to Prove):**
- Incompetence or inability to perform role
- Requires extensive documentation
- Must show warnings and improvement plans
- Very difficult to sustain in court
- Usually better to pay severance

**7. Repeated Violations:**
- Multiple written warnings for same issue
- Progressive discipline documented
- Reasonable opportunity to improve given

**Critical Requirements for Just Cause:**
- **Immediate termination** (cannot wait weeks)
- **Written notice** stating specific cause
- **Documentation** supporting the cause
- **Proportionate** to the violation
- **Consistent** application of policies

**Burden of Proof:**
- Employer must prove just cause in court
- Employee presumed right (pro operario principle)
- Insufficient evidence = wrongful termination
- Penalties: Reinstatement + back pay + damages

**Best Practice:**
Most startups pay severance rather than fight just cause battle in court. Only terminate for cause in egregious situations (theft, violence, gross misconduct).

### Probation Period

**Legal Probation:**
- First 3 months of employment
- Either party can terminate without severance
- No preaviso or antig√ºedad required
- Must still pay accrued vacation and 13th month
- Must be stated in employment contract

**After Probation:**
- Full severance protections apply
- Cannot extend probation beyond 3 months
- If no probation stated, full rights from day 1

**Best Practice:**
Always include 3-month probation in contract. Use this time to evaluate fit thoroughly.

### Interview Process Design

**Recommended Startup Interview Flow:**

**Stage 1: Initial Screen (30 min, virtual)**
- Recruiter or founder
- Culture fit assessment
- Role expectations alignment
- Salary expectations check
- Schedule: Within 24-48 hours of application

**Stage 2: Technical/Skills Assessment**
- Engineers: Coding challenge or take-home project (4-6 hours max)
- Sales: Role-play or case study
- Product: Product sense questions
- 45-60 minutes
- Evaluate core competencies

**Stage 3: Team Interview (60-90 min)**
- Meet potential teammates (2-3 people)
- Deep dive on experience and skills
- Collaboration and communication assessment
- Culture fit evaluation

**Stage 4: Founder/Leadership Interview (45-60 min)**
- Vision alignment
- Long-term career goals
- Final questions and concerns
- Close the candidate (sell the opportunity)

**Stage 5: Reference Checks**
- 2-3 professional references
- Focus on: performance, teamwork, growth trajectory
- Check for red flags

**Total Timeline:**
- Target: 1-2 weeks from application to offer
- Fast process = competitive advantage in tight market
- Communication after each stage critical

**Interview Best Practices:**
- Structured questions (ask same questions to all candidates)
- Diverse interview panel (avoid bias)
- Take detailed notes (for comparison and legal protection)
- Assess for potential, not just past experience
- Clear scoring rubric (1-5 scale on key criteria)
- Avoid illegal questions (age, marital status, pregnancy, etc.)

### Contractor vs Employee Classification

**True Independent Contractor Characteristics:**

‚úÖ **Contractor:**
- Services provided to multiple clients
- Controls own schedule and work methods
- Uses own equipment and tools
- Bears business risk (profit or loss)
- Invoices for services rendered
- No fixed schedule or location requirement
- Temporary or project-based work
- Specialized expertise for specific task
- Can delegate or subcontract work

‚ùå **Not Contractor (Actually Employee):**
- Works exclusively for one company
- Follows company schedule and instructions
- Uses company equipment and office
- Receives fixed monthly payment
- Integrated into company operations
- Indefinite ongoing relationship
- Performs core business functions
- Reports to manager like employees

**Misclassification Consequences:**

If classified as contractor but actually employee:
- Treated as employee from day 1
- Company owes: 13th month, vacation, CSS contributions (retroactive)
- Severance owed if terminated
- Fines from MITRADEL: $500-$5,000 per violation
- Employee can sue for benefits + damages
- Reputational damage

**Safe Classification Test:**
```
Answer honestly:

1. Works exclusively for our company?              YES = Employee risk
2. Follows our schedule/work hours?                YES = Employee risk
3. Uses our equipment/office?                      YES = Employee risk
4. Receives fixed monthly payment?                 YES = Employee risk
5. Integrated into team operations?                YES = Employee risk
6. Performs ongoing core business functions?       YES = Employee risk
7. Cannot delegate or subcontract?                 YES = Employee risk

If 4+ YES answers ‚Üí High employee misclassification risk
```

**Best Practice:**
When in doubt, classify as employee. Cost of misclassification > cost of proper employment.

## Common HR Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### Top 10 HR Mistakes Panama Startups Make

**1. Misclassifying Employees as Contractors**
‚ùå Problem: Treating employees as independent contractors to save costs
‚úÖ Solution: Properly classify based on control, integration, and exclusivity tests
**Cost:** Back pay + CSS contributions + penalties + severance

**2. No Written Employment Contracts**
‚ùå Problem: Hiring without signed contracts
‚úÖ Solution: Sign contract within 15 days of start, keep copies
**Cost:** Presumption of indefinite contract + employee-favorable terms

**3. Not Accruing 13th Month**
‚ùå Problem: Not setting aside money for 13th month payments
‚úÖ Solution: Accrue 1/12 of monthly salary each month
**Cost:** Cash flow crisis + late payment penalties

**4. Ignoring Vacation Accrual**
‚ùå Problem: Not tracking or paying accrued vacation
‚úÖ Solution: Track monthly accrual (2.5 days/month), pay at termination
**Cost:** 125% of owed vacation + legal fees if sued

**5. Inadequate Termination Documentation**
‚ùå Problem: Firing for "performance" without documentation
‚úÖ Solution: Written warnings, PIP, thorough documentation before termination
**Cost:** Wrongful termination claim = reinstatement + back pay

**6. Using Fixed-Term Contracts for Permanent Roles**
‚ùå Problem: Repeatedly using 1-year contracts to avoid severance
‚úÖ Solution: Use indefinite contracts for permanent positions
**Cost:** Automatic conversion to indefinite + penalties

**7. Not Paying Severance Immediately**
‚ùå Problem: Delaying final payments after termination
‚úÖ Solution: Pay all final amounts within 3 business days max
**Cost:** 125% of owed amounts + interest + legal fees

**8. Poor Onboarding**
‚ùå Problem: No structured onboarding process
‚úÖ Solution: 30-60-90 day onboarding plan, buddy system
**Cost:** Low productivity + higher turnover (20-30% leave in first 6 months)

**9. No Performance Management**
‚ùå Problem: No feedback until annual review (or never)
‚úÖ Solution: Weekly 1-on-1s, quarterly OKR reviews, annual formal reviews
**Cost:** Disengaged employees + higher turnover

**10. Neglecting Company Culture**
‚ùå Problem: Treating culture as "nice to have" not priority
‚úÖ Solution: Define values, hire/fire based on culture fit, invest in team building
**Cost:** Toxic culture = turnover + inability to attract talent
