```

LTV by Segment:
- Small Enterprise: $97,500 (3.2 year lifetime)
- Mid-Market: $185,400 (3.6 year lifetime)
- Large Enterprise: $468,000 (4.8 year lifetime)

```
LTV:CAC Ratios:
- Small Enterprise: 11.5:1
- Mid-Market: 10.0:1
- Large Enterprise: 25.3:1
- Blended Average: 18.0:1

Target Benchmarks:
✓ LTV:CAC > 3:1 (Achieved: 18:1)
✓ Payback < 12 months (Achieved: 8.3 months)
✓ Gross Margin > 70% (Achieved: 78%)
✓ Net Revenue Retention > 100% (Achieved: 118%)

## 8. Term Sheet Key Terms Analysis

### Standard Series A Term Sheet Structure

**Summary of Terms:**
```
Company: [Company Name]
Investor: [Lead Investor Name]
Security: Series A Preferred Stock
Amount: $7,500,000
Pre-Money Valuation: $15,000,000
Post-Money Valuation: $22,500,000
Price per Share: $2.50
Number of Shares: 3,000,000
Investor Ownership: 33.3%
Option Pool: 15% (post-money)
```

### Term-by-Term Analysis

**1. Liquidation Preference**

**Market Standard:**
```
1x Non-Participating Preferred

Meaning: In a sale/liquidation, investors get their money
back first ($7.5M), then remaining proceeds shared pro-rata
with common stock based on ownership percentages.

Example Exit Scenarios:
$15M exit: Investors get $7.5M, founders get $7.5M
$45M exit: Investors get $15M (33.3%), founders get $30M (66.7%)
$100M exit: Investors get $33.3M, founders get $66.7M
```

**Aggressive Alternative (Avoid):**
```
1x Participating Preferred

Meaning: Investors get money back PLUS pro-rata share
$45M exit: Investors get $7.5M + $12.5M = $20M (44.4%)
Founders get: $25M (55.6%)

Impact: Significantly worse for founders in moderate exits
```

**Founder-Friendly Alternative:**
```
1x Non-Participating with Cap

Meaning: Participation capped at 2-3x return
Prevents investors from getting both liquidation preference
and high ownership percentage in moderate exits
```

**Analysis:** Insist on 1x non-participating. This is market standard and fair protection for investors while preserving founder upside.

**2. Anti-Dilution Protection**

**Market Standard:**
```
Broad-Based Weighted Average Anti-Dilution

Formula: Adjusts conversion price based on weighted average
of new issuance price and existing price, considering all
outstanding shares and options.

Impact: Minimal dilution protection in moderate down rounds,
reasonable protection in severe down rounds.

Example:
Current Price: $2.50/share
Down Round Price: $1.25/share (50% down)
Adjustment: ~$2.12/share (15% protection)
```

**Aggressive Alternative (Avoid):**
```
Full Ratchet Anti-Dilution

Impact: Conversion price drops to lowest price of any future round
Example: $2.50 → $1.25 (full protection to $1.25)
Result: Massive dilution to founders and employees
```

**Founder-Friendly Alternative:**
```
Narrow-Based Weighted Average

Impact: Only considers outstanding stock, not options/warrants
Result: Better protection for investors, more founder dilution
```

**Analysis:** Broad-based weighted average is fair and market standard. Avoid full ratchet at all costs - it can destroy founder ownership in down rounds.

**3. Board of Directors**

**Recommended Structure:**
```
5-Person Board:
- 2 Founder Directors (CEO + Co-Founder)
- 2 Investor Directors (Lead Investor + Major Investor)
- 1 Independent Director (mutually agreed)

Voting Requirements:
- Board majority (3 of 5) for most decisions
- Founder consent required for CEO hiring/firing
- Investor consent required for liquidation events
```

**Alternative Structures:**
```
3-Person Board (Earlier Stage):
- 1 Founder (CEO)
- 1 Investor (Lead)
- 1 Independent

7-Person Board (Later Stage):
- 3 Founders/Management
- 3 Investors (multiple VCs)
- 1 Independent
```

**Red Flags:**
```
❌ Investor Board Control (>50% investor directors)
❌ No Independent Director (3-person with investor majority)
❌ Super-majority requirements favor one party heavily
❌ Board seats tied to specific individuals who might leave
```

**Analysis:** Balanced board is critical. Founders should maintain effective control while giving investors appropriate governance rights.

**4. Protective Provisions**

**Standard Protective Provisions (Acceptable):**
```
Investor consent required for:
✓ Liquidation, dissolution, or sale of company
✓ Amendment of charter or bylaws that affects investor rights
✓ Authorization of new equity securities
✓ Payment of dividends or distributions
✓ Redemption or repurchase of shares
✓ Approval of annual budget and material changes
✓ Transactions with affiliates >$50K
✓ Changes to Board size or voting agreements
```

**Expanded Provisions (Pushback Required):**
```
❌ Hiring/firing of CEO or key executives
❌ Individual employee compensation >$150K
❌ Any acquisition or investment >$100K
❌ Approval of customer contracts >$500K
❌ Changes to intellectual property strategy
❌ Opening new offices or international expansion
❌ Selection of auditors, lawyers, or advisors
❌ Approval of press releases or communications
```

**Analysis:** Standard protective provisions protect legitimate investor interests. Push back on operational control provisions that limit day-to-day management flexibility.

**5. Pay-to-Play Provision**

**Standard Structure:**
```
If investor doesn't participate pro-rata in qualified financing:
- Series A converts to common stock
- Loses anti-dilution protection
- Loses special board designation
- Retains liquidation preference
```

**Aggressive Structure (Avoid):**
```
Non-participating investor:
- Loses ALL investor rights
- Series A converts to common stock
- Loses liquidation preference
- No board representation
```

**Analysis:** Mild pay-to-play provisions ensure investor commitment to future rounds. Avoid versions that completely strip rights.

**6. Drag-Along Rights**

**Standard Terms:**
```
Threshold: 75% of outstanding shares (including common)
Scope: Forces all shareholders to participate in approved sale
Protections:
- Market price and terms
- No personal liability for shareholders
- Same terms for all shareholders in same class
```

**Variations:**
```
60% Threshold: More aggressive, easier to trigger
51% Threshold: Too low, gives too much control
90% Threshold: Too high, makes drag-along ineffective

Board + Majority: Requires both board approval AND
shareholder majority (double protection)
```

**Analysis:** 75% threshold with proper protections is fair balance. Ensures alignment while protecting minority interests.

**7. Information Rights**

**Standard Requirements:**
```
Monthly Reporting:
- Financial statements (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Operational metrics dashboard (MRR, churn, pipeline)
- Customer and employee updates

Quarterly Reporting:
- Board packages with detailed analysis
- Budget vs. actual performance
- Forward-looking projections

Annual Requirements:
- Audited financial statements (if available)
- Annual budget approval
- Strategic plan updates
```

**Reasonable Additions:**
```
- Access to management for reasonable consultation
- Advance notice of board meetings (5-10 business days)
- Inspection rights with reasonable notice
- Annual investor meeting or call
```

**Problematic Requests:**
```
❌ Weekly reporting requirements
❌ Access to customer contracts and data
❌ Approval rights over disclosure content
❌ Rights to attend all management meetings
❌ Individual customer performance data
```

**Analysis:** Reasonable information rights help investors add value and monitor performance. Avoid requirements that create excessive administrative burden.

**8. Registration Rights**

**Standard Structure:**
```
Demand Rights:
- 1-2 demand registrations after earlier of (i) 3 years
  or (ii) 6 months after IPO
- Minimum offering size: $15-25M

Piggyback Rights:
- Right to include shares in company-initiated offerings
- Pro-rata allocation based on ownership
- Cutback provisions favor company shares first

S-3 Rights:
- Unlimited shelf registration rights if company qualifies
- Minimum offering size: $2-5M
```

**Key Negotiation Points:**
```
Registration Expenses:
- Company pays all costs except underwriting discounts
- Investor pays pro-rata for marketing/roadshow costs

Lock-up Provisions:
- 180-day lock-up for all shareholders post-IPO
- Management lock-up may be shorter (90-120 days)

Termination:
- Rights terminate when investor owns <1% of company
- Or 3-5 years after IPO registration
```

**Analysis:** Standard registration rights are important for investor liquidity. Terms should be reasonable and not overly burdensome for company.

**9. Option Pool**

**Sizing Considerations:**
```
Pre-Money vs. Post-Money:
- Pre-Money: Dilutes founders only
- Post-Money: Dilutes both founders and investors

Typical Sizes:
- Series A: 15-20% post-money
- Pre-Seed: 10-15% post-money
- Later Rounds: 5-10% post-money

18-Month Hiring Plan:
Calculate actual needs based on hiring plan
Include senior hires at market rates
Account for promotion-based grants
```

**Negotiation Strategy:**
```
1. Prepare detailed hiring plan with levels and timing
2. Research market compensation data for roles
3. Calculate actual equity needs vs. proposed pool
4. Negotiate based on specific requirements
5. Consider post-money treatment to share dilution
```

**Analysis:** Right-size option pool based on actual hiring needs. Avoid padding that creates unnecessary dilution for founders.
