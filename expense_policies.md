# Expense Policies & Approval Workflows
## Enterprise Documentation SaaS - Panama S.A.

### Policy Overview

This document establishes expense management policies, approval workflows, and controls to ensure proper financial governance while supporting business growth.

### General Expense Principles

#### Core Guidelines
1. **Business Purpose Required**: All expenses must have legitimate business purpose
2. **Documentation Mandatory**: Receipts and justification required for all expenses
3. **Pre-approval Required**: Expenses above thresholds require advance approval
4. **Reasonable and Necessary**: Expenses must be reasonable and necessary for business
5. **Compliance**: Must comply with Panama tax laws and IFRS accounting standards

#### Prohibited Expenses
- Personal expenses of any nature
- Gifts to customers exceeding $100 per person per year
- Political contributions or lobbying
- Fines, penalties, or legal settlements
- Entertainment deemed excessive or inappropriate
- Travel for personal purposes

### Expense Categories & Approval Limits

#### 1. Technology & Software

**Allowed Expenses**:
- Development tools and software licenses
- Cloud hosting and infrastructure (AWS, GCP, Azure)
- Security software and services
- Productivity software (Office 365, Slack, etc.)
- Code repositories and CI/CD tools

**Approval Limits**:
- Up to $500/month: Department head approval
- $501-$2,500/month: CFO approval
- Over $2,500/month: CEO approval
- Annual contracts >$10K: Board approval (if over budget)

**Required Documentation**:
- Vendor quote or invoice
- Business justification
- License terms and renewal dates
- Security/compliance review (for SaaS tools)

#### 2. Professional Services

**Allowed Expenses**:
- Legal services
- Accounting and audit fees
- Management consulting
- Technical consulting
- Tax advisory services

**Approval Limits**:
- Up to $2,500: CFO approval
- $2,501-$10,000: CEO approval
- Over $10,000: CEO + Board approval

**Required Documentation**:
- Engagement letter or statement of work
- Hourly rates or fixed fee structure
- Scope of work and deliverables
- Conflict of interest disclosure

#### 3. Marketing & Sales

**Allowed Expenses**:
- Digital advertising (Google, LinkedIn, etc.)
- Trade shows and conferences
- Marketing technology stack
- Content creation and design
- Lead generation services

**Approval Limits**:
- Up to $1,000/month: Marketing Manager
- $1,001-$5,000/month: VP Sales/Marketing
- Over $5,000/month: CEO approval

**Special Rules**:
- Conference attendance: Max $3,000 per person per event
- Entertainment: Max $200 per customer meeting
- Swag/promotional materials: Pre-approved budget only

#### 4. Travel & Entertainment

**Travel Policy**:
- **Flight Class**: Economy for flights under 6 hours, business for longer
- **Hotels**: Reasonable business-class hotels, max $300/night in major cities
- **Meals**: Reasonable business meals, max $100/day for meals alone
- **Ground Transport**: Reasonable transport, prefer ride-sharing over taxis

**Entertainment Guidelines**:
- **Customer Meals**: Max $150 per person for customer meetings
- **Team Events**: Quarterly team events, max $75 per employee
- **Client Entertainment**: Pre-approval required for >$200 events

**Approval Requirements**:
- Domestic travel <$1,500: Manager approval
- Domestic travel >$1,500: Department head approval
- International travel: CFO approval
- Customer entertainment >$200: Department head approval

#### 5. Office & Facilities

**Allowed Expenses**:
- Office rent and utilities
- Internet and telecommunications
- Office supplies and equipment
- Kitchen/break room supplies
- Security services

**Approval Limits**:
- Routine supplies <$500: Office manager
- Equipment $501-$2,500: Department head
- Furniture/major equipment >$2,500: CFO approval
- Lease agreements: CEO approval

#### 6. Personnel & HR

**Allowed Expenses**:
- Recruiting fees
- Training and development
- Employee benefits
- Team building activities
- Employee recognition

**Approval Requirements**:
- Training per employee <$1,000: Manager approval
- Recruiting fees: HR Director approval
- Team events: Department head approval
- Employee recognition: Manager approval

### Expense Reporting Process

#### 1. Employee Expense Reports

**Monthly Submission Required**:
- Submit by 5th of following month
- Use approved expense management system
- Include digital copies of all receipts
- Provide business justification for each expense

**Required Information**:
- Date and amount of expense
- Vendor/merchant name
- Business purpose and attendees (if applicable)
- Project or department allocation
- Receipt or invoice

**Review Process**:
1. Employee submits expense report
2. Manager reviews and approves
3. Accounting reviews for compliance
4. CFO spot-checks reports monthly
5. Payment processed within 10 business days

#### 2. Corporate Card Expenses

**Card Assignment**:
- CEO, CFO, VP Sales, VP Engineering: $5,000 limits
- Department heads: $2,500 limits
- Selected employees: $1,000 limits

**Monthly Reconciliation**:
- Cardholders submit receipts by month-end
- Accounting reconciles all transactions
- Unapproved expenses deducted from payroll
- Repeated violations result in card cancellation

#### 3. Vendor Invoice Processing

**Invoice Requirements**:
- Valid tax invoice format (Panama requirements)
- Purchase order or contract reference
- Proper coding to chart of accounts
- Approval signature from authorized person

**Payment Process**:
1. Invoice received and logged
2. Three-way match (PO, receipt, invoice)
3. Department approval confirmation
4. CFO review for >$2,500 invoices
5. Payment within terms (typically 30 days)

### Technology Expense Guidelines

#### SaaS Software Procurement

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Business necessity and ROI
- Security and compliance requirements
- Integration capabilities
- Scalability and pricing model
- Vendor stability and support

**Approval Process**:
1. Department identifies need
2. IT security review (if applicable)
3. Legal review of terms >$10K annually
4. CFO approval within spending limits
5. Contract execution and setup

**Ongoing Management**:
- Quarterly usage review
- Annual contract renewal evaluation
- Vendor performance assessment
- License optimization (add/remove users)

#### Hardware & Equipment

**Laptop/Computer Standards**:
- Engineers: High-performance development machines
- Sales/Marketing: Standard business laptops
- Administrative: Basic business computers
- Replace every 3 years or as needed

**Mobile Device Policy**:
- Company provides phones for customer-facing roles
- BYOD policy for others with monthly stipend
- Mobile device management (MDM) required

### International Expense Considerations

#### Multi-Currency Transactions

**Preferred Currencies**: USD (primary), EUR (for European vendors)

**Exchange Rate Policy**:
- Use daily rate for transaction recording
- Monthly revaluation for outstanding payables
- Hedge significant exposures >$25K

#### Cross-Border Payments

**Payment Methods**:
- Wire transfers for large payments >$5K
- International corporate cards for small amounts
- PayPal/Payoneer for small vendors where appropriate

**Documentation Requirements**:
- All international payments >$1K require contracts
- Tax withholding compliance where applicable
- Transfer pricing documentation for related parties

### Tax Compliance & Reporting

#### Panama Tax Considerations

**ITBMS (VAT) Treatment**:
- Panama vendors: 7% ITBMS on most services
- International vendors: Generally no ITBMS
- Proper tax receipts required for deductions

**Deductibility Requirements**:
- Business purpose documentation
- Valid tax invoices
- Timely recording in accounting system
- Proper approval and authorization

#### Territorial Tax Optimization

**Expense Allocation**:
- Allocate expenses between Panama and foreign source activities
- Maintain supporting documentation for allocation
- Ensure arms-length pricing for intercompany charges

### Control Environment

#### Segregation of Duties

**Purchase Process**:
- Requisition: Department head
- Purchase order: Procurement/Finance
- Receipt: Requesting department
- Invoice approval: Department head
- Payment: Finance/CFO

**Review Controls**:
- Monthly expense report reviews
- Quarterly vendor payment analysis
- Annual policy compliance audit
- Board oversight of large expenditures

#### Monthly Reporting

**Expense Analytics**:
- Budget vs actual by category
- Variance analysis and explanations
- Department-level spending summaries
- Vendor concentration analysis
- Policy exception reporting

### Policy Exceptions & Escalations

#### Exception Request Process

**When Exceptions Needed**:
- Expenses exceeding approval limits
- Non-standard vendor terms
- Emergency purchases
- International travel changes

**Approval Process**:
1. Submit exception request with justification
2. CFO review and recommendation
3. CEO approval for material exceptions
4. Board notification for >$25K exceptions

#### Emergency Procedures

**After-Hours/Weekend Expenses**:
- Use corporate card for emergencies
- Obtain retroactive approval within 24 hours
- Document emergency nature and business need

### Training & Communication

#### New Employee Orientation
- Expense policy training within first week
- Expense system setup and training
- Manager review of approval authorities
- Quarterly policy updates and training

#### Policy Updates
- Annual policy review and updates
- Communicate changes via all-hands meeting
- Update approval workflows in expense system
- Training on new requirements as needed

### Audit & Compliance

#### Internal Audits
- Quarterly expense policy compliance reviews
- Annual vendor management audit
- Monthly corporate card transaction reviews
- Random receipt and justification audits

#### External Audits
- Annual auditor expense testing
- Transfer pricing documentation reviews
- Tax compliance verification
- Corporate governance assessments
